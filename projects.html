Intelligent Virtual Assistants and Data Privacy

This study explores Intelligent Virtual Assistants (IVAs), their functionality, and the evolving process of linking user devices to servers with deep neural networks. IVAs, adept at responding to user queries, have adapted their procedures to address rising concerns about privacy and consumer rights. Many IVA companies have restricted third-party access to speech data, enhancing data security. However, opacity persists in technology companies' data usage practices, necessitating user understanding without navigating complex privacy policies. The research highlights the industry's heavy reliance on advertisements, urging transparent data privacy practices and enhanced user protection. In conclusion, the study calls for increased accountability in the technology sector, focusing on addressing antitrust violations. By exposing the challenges related to IVAs and data privacy, the research emphasizes the need for informed user consent, robust privacy measures, and heightened transparency to uphold ethical standards in the digital era.

UN Sustainability Goals Lesson

In this interactive presentation centered on the first three UN Sustainable Development Goals (No Poverty, Zero Hunger, and Good Health & Wellbeing), students participate in activities such as a warm-up discussion, a thought-provoking "Steppin' Game," collaborative Venn diagram creation, and anonymous reflections. These activities encourage active participation and critical thinking, enabling students to explore the concepts deeply. The session aims to deepen students' understanding of the SDGs while fostering engagement and reflection in a concise and engaging format.
